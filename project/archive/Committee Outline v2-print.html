Workshop Outline
AI Best Practices with Claude.

A 60-minute hands-on workshop for learning and development professionals. What's different from an AI 101 session: every attendee leaves having built something real, and takes home a pack they can use the next day.

What changed from the first draft Same commitments, sharper arc. The session no longer opens with “what is AI” (this audience knows); it opens with what makes Claude different, then spends most of the hour on three capabilities that reshape L&D work: Artifacts (build real, interactive outputs), Projects & Skills (so Claude remembers your context), and Connectors (Canva, featured live). We also touch briefly on Claude Designer — a week-old capability every L&D professional should at least know exists. Three activities, three takeaways.

OutcomesWhat attendees leave able to do.

Outcomes are observable by the end of the session or within one week.

  • Write a Claude-shaped prompt. Apply at least three specific moves (clarifying questions, success criteria, pushback, critique-revise, one-task-only).
  • Ship one working Artifact. A quiz, decision tree, or practice simulation they built in the session — not a draft, a live thing.
  • Set up one Project. With files, instructions, and a first real conversation — so Claude remembers their context on Monday.
  • Name the difference between a prompt, a Project, a Skill, and a Connector — and choose the right one for their next recurring task.
  • Form an informed opinion on Claude Designer and what it means for their team's e-authoring stack.
  • Apply three guardrails for responsible use in L&D (data, attribution, verification).

The outlineSix acts, sixty minutes.

Act One 10 min

Why Claude (not AI 101).

Position the hour. This audience already knows AI — we go straight to what makes Claude different and why it matters for their work.

  • The 60-minute agreement (ground rules, interruption policy)
  • Facilitator framing: explorer, not guru
  • Four things Claude does differently: long documents, Artifacts, voice, Projects
  • Five prompt moves that punch above their weight
  • The 3-use rule: prompt → saved prompt → Project or Skill
  • Activity 01 (6 min): Write a Claude-shaped prompt for a real task you have this week
DeliverableTwo prompts shared out loud; everyone has a working prompt for a real task.
Act Two 20 min

What Claude builds — Artifacts.

The capability that most reframes what L&D can ship solo: real, interactive outputs, not just text drafts.

  • What an Artifact is: a working thing in a side panel — not a description of the thing
  • Six Artifacts you can ship this week: job aids, self-checks, widgets, simulations, microsites, games
  • What's on the free tier (everything needed for the activity)
  • Activity 02 (12 min): Build a working Artifact — one of three options
    • A 5-question self-check on a topic learners struggle with
    • An interactive decision tree for a recurring judgment call
    • A conversation-practice sim (Claude plays the other person)
DeliverableEvery attendee ships a live Artifact link and shares it with the person next to them.
Act Three 15 min

How Claude remembers you — Projects.

The under-invested capability. Most people type the same context every chat; Projects fix that.

  • What a Project is: files + instructions that every chat inside inherits
  • Writing instructions that stick: role, audience, non-negotiables, voice sample
  • A fill-in-the-blank Instructions template (in the take-home pack)
  • CLAUDE.md, demystified — one slide, because attendees will hear the term
  • Activity 03 (8 min): Set up one real Project — name, files, instructions, first chat
DeliverableEveryone has a Project ready to do real work on Monday.
Act Four 8 min

What Claude connects to — Skills & Connectors.

Beyond the chat window. How Claude becomes part of a workflow.

  • Skills: procedures Claude runs reliably; Anthropic's built-in library is now on free
  • Connectors: the growing library; mix of free and paid-tier availability
  • Live demo: Canva connector — turn a learning objective into a one-page asset, in real time, from chat
DeliverableA concrete, visible example of Claude producing polished work in a tool the audience already uses.
Act Five 5 min

The new thing, and the real talk.

A brief preview and an honest reckoning — both essential for L&D credibility.

  • Claude Designer (screenshot tour, 2 min): a Canva-partnered capability released days before the workshop. Framed as “pay attention to this” — may reshape parts of the e-authoring stack (Articulate, Rise, Vyond)
  • What Claude gets wrong: citations, your context, recency, deep SME judgment, agreeableness, modeling real learners
  • Responsible use — three guardrails: data (don't paste what you wouldn't email), attribution (you are the author), verification (primary sources for anything a learner will cite)
DeliverableA realistic, trustworthy picture of the tool — not a sales pitch.
Act Six 2 min + Q&A

Your turn.

Momentum past the hour. A clear 7-day plan, a designed take-home pack, and an invitation to keep going.

  • Facilitator's practice — six real things I've built on the Pro plan ($20/mo), plus what I've deliberately not done (agents, Cowork, Claude Desktop). Credibility through honesty.
  • Your 7-day plan — one small action per day
  • Take-home pack overview — what's in it, how to use it
  • Q&A — including “I don't know” answers where warranted
DeliverableEvery attendee leaves with a plan, a pack, and a working Artifact.

Appendix AWhat's in the take-home pack.

A designed PDF emailed the evening of the workshop. Six parts plus a bonus appendix.

Part 1The L&D Prompt Library — 12 ready-to-paste prompts for assessments, feedback, SME conversations, microlearning, and more. Each names the job and why it works.
Part 2The 7-Day Challenge — one small action per day, printable and trackable.
Part 3Ten Habits — small moves that compound: the posture of heavy Claude users.
Part 4Reflection Sheet — six questions to sit with after the seven days.
Part 5Project Instructions Template — the fill-in-the-blank starter, with the four places people under-invest.
Part 6Skills Playbook — decision tree for prompt vs. Project vs. Skill vs. Connector, plus four Skills worth turning on first.
AppendixClaude Code for the Curious — facilitator's exact setup (VS Code extension, no terminal), six-step getting-started, first-project prompt, honest caveats. For attendees who want to go further.

Appendix BLogistics & risk.

Room setupTables or seminar-style seating. Screen visible from every seat. No need for breakout rooms; activities are at-table.
Required of attendeesLaptop (phones workable but suboptimal for Artifacts), a Claude.ai account (free fine), and the pre-work read (10 minutes).
Pre-work sentOne week before: account setup, one sample prompt to try, one L&D task to bring
Backup planIf the Canva connector is unavailable live, a pre-recorded 90-second screen capture stands in. Activities 01–03 don't require the connector.
Data handlingAttendees are briefed before Activity 02 and 03 to avoid pasting PII, salary data, or identifying performance data. Facilitator models this in every demo.
Scope boundariesNot a session on: agents, Claude Desktop, Cowork, Claude Code for engineering teams. These are named and bounded up front; curious attendees get the Claude Code appendix.

Appendix CA note on voice.

The facilitator is on the $20/month Pro plan and has deliberately held back from agents, Cowork, and the higher-tier features. That is the point of view of this workshop. Attendees don't need a power-user's toolkit to do meaningful work — they need an honest guide who has shipped real things with the same plan they can afford.

What attendees will hear: “Here's what I've built. Here's what I haven't tried yet. Here's how you can do the first thing this week.” That authenticity is a feature, not a caveat.
